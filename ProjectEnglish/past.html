Simple Past

Format : Sub + verb (V2 form)

When we use
  • Past Action
  • Regular and Irregular Verbs
  • Time Indicators
  • No Connection to Present
Example
  • Yesterday, Sarah finished her homework and then went to bed. (Past Action)
  • James played basketball with his friends. (Regular Verb)
  • Yesterday, we went to the beach. (Irregular Verb)
  • Last week, we visited our grandparents. (Time Indicators)
  • She studied hard for the exam last month, but now it's over and she's relaxing. (No Connection to Present)
Keywords

Important Keywords:
   last (week, month, year), previous (day, month, year), once

Past Continuous

Format : Sub + was/ were + verb + ing

I = was

You/ We/ They = were

He/ She/ It = was

When we use
  • Ongoing action in the past.
  • Interrupted action.
  • Parallel action.
  • Background action.
  • Polite requests.
Example
  • I was studying when the phone rang. (Onging action)
  • She was cooking dinner when the guests arrived. (interrupted action)
  • While they were playing outside, their parents were preparing dinner. (Parallel action)
  • The rain was falling as we were having a picnic. (Background action)
  • I was wondering if you could help me with this. (Polite action)
Keywords

Important Keywords:
   While, When, as, Yesterday, This time yesterday.

Past Perfect

Format : had + [past participle]

When we use
  • Completed action before something in the past.
  • Emphasis on result.
  • Multiple past actions.
  • Constract with past simple.
  • Unfulfilled past.
Example
  • She had already finished her homework when her friends came over. (Completed actions before something on the past)
  • By the time we reached the cinema, the movie had already started. (Emphasis on result)
  • They had eaten dinner, and then they went to the concert. (Multiple past actions)
  • He had visited that city before, so he knew his way around. (Constract with past simple)
  • I regretted that I had not studied more for the exam. (Unfulfilled past)
Keywords

Important Keywords:
   Before,Already, had, By the time, until, after, Never before.

Past Perfect Continuous

Format : Sub + had been + verb (ing) + O

When we use
  • Duration before past event.
  • cause of past event.
  • Interrupted past action.
  • Emphasis on duration.
  • Unfinished time period up to Another action.
Example
  • They had been living there for ten years before they moved. (Duration before past event)
  • She was tired because she had been working all day. (cause of past event)
  • I had been studying when the phone rang. (Interrupted past action)
  • By the time we arrived,they had been waiting for over an hour. (Emphasis on duration)
  • He had been running a marathon before he twisted his ankle. (Unfinished time period up to Another action)
Keywords

Important Keywords:
   When, for, since, and before
